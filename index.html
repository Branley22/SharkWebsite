The Fearsome Predator of the Sea: The Great White

Fun facts about this Great White Shark!

  1. The Great White is at the top of the food chain with a few threats being other larger sharks, orcas, and humans.

  2. While white sharks are young, they mostly feed on smaller fish. As they grow, they start to feed more on larger mammals like small whales.

  3. Many great white sharks on average grow to 15th feet and some have been recoreded to grow up to 20 feet.

  4. These sharks are many rows of teeth estimating to be in the thousands. Even if a tooth falls out, they are quickly replaced.

  5. These sharks are located warm temperature waters.

  6. White sharks take a single bite on humans then retreat out of curiosity. Unfortunately, for the human this can be a minor injury or a fatal injury.

  7. Great whites are territorial animals that assume dominance over feeding areas.

  8. Prior to birth, the young feed on undeveloped eggs and their unborn siblings.

  9. Humans hunt white sharks for many reasons such as their fin and because they are a good fish to catch.

  10. White sharks have evolved for million of years and will continue to do so.
